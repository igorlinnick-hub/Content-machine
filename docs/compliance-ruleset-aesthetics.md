# Compliance Ruleset — Medical Aesthetics v1.0

> **Scope:** Botox / Dysport / dermal fillers / skin treatments / cosmetic injectables.
> This ruleset applies to Hawaii Wellness Clinic content for Dr. Made's aesthetics practice.
> Enforced automatically by the AI compliance gate before any post is approved.

---

## 1. Why these rules exist

**FDA — drug vs. device claims.** Injectable neuromodulators (Botox, Dysport) are FDA-**approved drugs** for specific cosmetic indications only. Dermal fillers (Juvederm, Restylane, Sculptra, etc.) are FDA-**cleared devices** for soft-tissue augmentation. Neither are approved for disease treatment. Wording that implies otherwise is a drug misbranding violation (21 U.S.C. § 352).

**FTC — substantiation.** Any health or appearance-outcome claim needs "competent and reliable scientific evidence." Unsubstantiated = deceptive. "Results vary" does **not** fix an unsubstantiated typical-results claim — you must substantiate AND disclose generally expected results (16 CFR Part 255, updated 2023).

**FTC — before/after photos.** Must reflect typical patient results. An exceptional result photo without a "results not typical" disclosure (and substantiation of what IS typical) is deceptive. Material connections (free/discounted treatment for testimonials) must be disclosed.

**FDA — off-label use.** Physicians may use products off-label, but **marketing** off-label use is regulated. If you describe a use not in the FDA-approved labeling, do not claim "FDA-approved" for it.

## 2. Approved products & their limits

### Neuromodulators (Botox, Dysport, Xeomin, Jeuveau)

| Product | FDA-approved cosmetic indications |
|---|---|
| Botox (onabotulinumtoxinA) | Glabellar lines (frown lines), crow's feet, forehead lines |
| Dysport (abobotulinumtoxinA) | Glabellar lines |
| Xeomin (incobotulinumtoxinA) | Glabellar lines |
| Jeuveau (prabotulinumtoxinA) | Glabellar lines |

**Rules:**
- ✅ "FDA-approved for glabellar lines" — OK
- ✅ "Botox is FDA-approved to temporarily improve the appearance of frown lines" — OK
- ❌ "FDA-approved Botox treatment" (without naming the indication) — too broad, implies general approval
- ❌ "FDA-approved for [jaw slimming / neck / brow lift / migraine]" — off-label; omit "FDA-approved"
- ❌ "Botox treats migraines" — marketing off-label use; say "may be used off-label by physicians for..."

### Dermal Fillers (Juvederm, Restylane, Sculptra, Radiesse, etc.)

- Fillers are **FDA-cleared devices**, NOT FDA-approved drugs
- "FDA-cleared" is accurate; "FDA-approved" is not
- Never claim permanent results — HA fillers last months, Sculptra/Radiesse last longer but are not permanent
- Do not claim they "treat" skin conditions, diseases, or aging as a disease

**Rules:**
- ✅ "FDA-cleared hyaluronic acid filler" — OK
- ✅ "may restore volume and soften the appearance of lines" — OK
- ❌ "FDA-approved filler" — incorrect; use "FDA-cleared"
- ❌ "permanent results" / "eliminates wrinkles forever" — never
- ❌ "reverses aging" / "turns back the clock" — disease/structure claim, remove

### Skincare, Lasers, Energy-Based Devices

- Most do not carry broad FDA approval for cosmetic anti-aging claims
- Use: "clinically studied", "shown to improve skin texture", "may reduce the appearance of"
- Never: "FDA-approved to reduce wrinkles" unless you can cite the exact cleared indication

## 3. Rules the AI gate enforces (rule IDs)

### R-CLAIM-01 — No disease/structure/function cure claims
**Triggers:** "treats / cures / reverses / eliminates / restores / regenerates" applied to a condition, disease, or body structure.
**Reword to:** "may reduce the appearance of", "can soften", "may improve skin texture", "studies show improvement in"

Examples:
- ❌ "Botox treats forehead wrinkles" → ✅ "Botox may temporarily reduce the appearance of forehead lines"
- ❌ "fillers reverse signs of aging" → ✅ "fillers can restore volume and soften the appearance of lines"
- ❌ "eliminates crow's feet" → ✅ "may reduce the appearance of crow's feet"

### R-CLAIM-02 — No outcome guarantees
**Triggers:** "will work", "guaranteed", "100% effective", "always works", "you will see results", "miracle"
**Reword to:** hedged language — "many patients notice", "results vary", "typically"

### R-FDA-01 — Accurate FDA status only
**Triggers:** "FDA-approved" for off-label uses or for filler devices (which are cleared, not approved)
**Reword to:** use the correct term ("FDA-cleared" for devices; omit "FDA-approved" for off-label uses)

### R-EVIDENCE-01 — Label evidence stage
**Triggers:** naked statistics or study references without a qualifier
**Reword to:** add "in clinical studies", "in a study of X patients", "pilot data shows"

### R-PROMISE-01 — Mandatory hedging in therapeutic posts
**Triggers:** a post about a treatment with zero hedging phrases
**Fix:** add at least one: "may help", "can support", "many patients", "results vary", "individual results may differ", "talk to your provider"

### R-RESULTS-01 — Results must be typical or disclosed
**Triggers:** before/after framing that implies the result shown is typical without substantiation
**Fix:** add "results not typical" + describe generally expected outcomes, OR only feature outcomes you can substantiate as typical

---

## 4. What to always include

- ✅ At least one hedging phrase per treatment claim
- ✅ "Results vary" / "individual results may differ" when discussing outcomes
- ✅ Duration guidance must be qualified: "typically lasts 3–4 months", not "lasts 3–4 months"
- ✅ Consultation language: "schedule a consultation to discuss your goals"
- ✅ For before/after posts: disclose if model is compensated, if results are exceptional

---

## 5. What to never write

| ❌ Phrase | ✅ Alternative |
|---|---|
| "permanently removes wrinkles" | "may reduce the appearance of wrinkles for several months" |
| "FDA-approved filler" | "FDA-cleared dermal filler" |
| "Botox cures TMJ" | "Botox may be used off-label by physicians to help relax jaw muscles" |
| "you will look 10 years younger" | "many patients notice a refreshed, more youthful appearance" |
| "reverses aging" | "may soften signs of aging" |
| "eliminates fine lines" | "may reduce the appearance of fine lines" |
| "guaranteed results" | "most patients see visible improvement" |
| "no downtime, ever" | "minimal downtime for most patients" |

---

## 6. Mental-health / crisis content

Not typically relevant for aesthetics content. If a post touches on body image or self-esteem, tone must be empowering and body-positive — never imply that a person is defective or that treatment is required to be acceptable.
