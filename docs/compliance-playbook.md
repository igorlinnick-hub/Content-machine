## The one test for everything
**“Would a patient read this as a promise of what THEY will get?”** If yes → it's a claim → follow
the rules below. When unsure: **don't post — ask the compliance owner (Igor / medical director).**

These rules apply **everywhere**, not just scheduled posts: captions, **video scripts**, Stories &
Highlights, hashtags, alt-text/file names — **and real-time: comments, DMs, Manychat auto-replies**.

## 9 things that must NEVER be in a post
1. Claim a therapy **treats/cures/reverses** a disease or **regenerates/restores** a body part (stem cells, exosomes, biologics not FDA-approved for this — #1 trigger; FDA grace period ended May 2021, clinics have been sued).
2. Market **exosomes** as a therapy we offer (FDA Public Safety Notification Dec 2019 — no approved exosome products).
3. Say **“FDA-approved”** unless literally true — and never confuse **“cleared” vs “approved”** (devices like ExoMind/TMS are 510(k)-*cleared* for depression/OCD, not “focus/mood”; compounded GLP-1 is not approved).
4. Post **success numbers** (“85%”, “100%”) or **“clinically proven”** without a study on file.
5. **Guarantee** outcomes — “guaranteed/permanent/for good/lasting/cure”.
6. Publish a **testimonial that credits a result to an unapproved therapy** — and don't think a disclaimer fixes it (see below).
7. Post **fake / AI / employee / incentivized reviews**, or hide/gate only the positive ones (FTC Fake Reviews Rule, 16 CFR 465, 2024 — civil penalties).
8. Frame **off-label drugs as approved** — IV ketamine for depression, compounded GLP-1 (only Spravato is approved).
9. Use **before/after** / transformation visuals — incl. **Stories/Highlights**, hashtags, emojis, file names (implied claim = claim).

## The testimonial trap (FTC 16 CFR §255.1)
“It’s the patient’s opinion, not ours” is **wrong**. We are liable for any claim in a testimonial we
publish, and it can’t say what we couldn’t say (or substantiate) directly (§255.1(c)).
- ✅ OK: “The staff was caring, I’m glad I came, I feel like myself again.” (experience)
- ✕ NOT OK: “The stem cells healed my knee / my seizures are gone.” (medical result → our claim)
- **A “results vary” disclaimer does NOT cure it** — the 2009 Guides removed that safe harbor. For an unapproved therapy you simply can't make the claim. Employee/free-treatment endorsers must be disclosed (§255.5).

## Real-time messages (the most likely rake)
- **Never answer “does it work / will it cure X?” in comments or DMs.** Reply: “We can't discuss outcomes here — book a free consult and a clinician will help,” then route to a clinician.
- **Hide** (don't argue with) third-party comments crediting a result to our therapy.
- Every **Manychat auto-reply** template follows this checklist + has a named owner (audit monthly).
- **No staff/owner/family posing as a patient.** Real patient on camera → signed HIPAA media release first.
- If a post links to a landing page, the **page must follow these rules too**.
- **New therapy not in the table?** Default = **no efficacy claims** until a clinician + compliance owner add a row.

## Rules per therapy
- **Stem cells** — never tie to a result/disease/body part; don’t name in testimonials.
- **Exosomes** — keep out of public marketing entirely.
- **Biologics / BMAC / PRP** — “autologous” ≠ exempt (becomes a regulated drug if more-than-minimally manipulated or used non-homologously — the SVF court case). No disease/“regenerate the joint” claims, no stats.
- **GLP-1** — “FDA-approved” only for brand (Wegovy/Zepbound); compounded = “not FDA-approved”; no “keep it off for good”.
- **Ketamine** — off-label for mood/pain; say “physician-supervised, off-label”; avoid “breakthrough/cure”.
- **IV / NAD+** — wellness-support only; no “100% absorption”, no disease/longevity cure claims.
- **Hormones / peptides** — “may support”; no “reverses aging”, no disease treatment.
- **Devices (ExoMind/TMS)** — claim only the exact FDA-cleared indication.

## Green light (what we CAN do)
Education on a condition/mechanism · condition-awareness without a treatment promise · pure-experience
testimonials · doctor explainer videos · hedged language (“may support”, “designed to support”, “results
vary”) · “book a free consultation” CTA.

## Pre-publish checklist (tick every box)
- [ ] Passes the one test: a patient would NOT read it as a promise of what they'll get
- [ ] No disease/cure/“regenerate/restore” claim tied to a therapy
- [ ] No exosomes presented as a service
- [ ] “FDA-approved” only where literally true (devices = “cleared,” not “approved”)
- [ ] No success numbers / “clinically proven” without a study on file
- [ ] No guarantees (“permanent/for good/lasting/100%”)
- [ ] Testimonial = pure experience only (a disclaimer does NOT fix an efficacy claim)
- [ ] No fake/AI/employee/incentivized reviews; not gating only positive ones
- [ ] Off-label drugs labeled off-label, not “approved”
- [ ] No before/after — incl. Stories/Highlights, hashtags, emojis, file names
- [ ] Same rules applied to the video script, comments, DMs/Manychat, and any linked page
- [ ] When unsure → don't publish; ask the compliance owner

**Golden rule:** describe the therapy + science, invite a consultation — never promise an outcome,
never name a disease we “cure,” never let a testimonial say what we can’t.
